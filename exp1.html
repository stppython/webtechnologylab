The 7 Wonders of the World

The "New 7 Wonders of the World" is a campaign that aimed to create a new list of seven wonders of the world, selected through a global poll. These magnificent structures represent incredible human ingenuity, architectural prowess, and cultural significance. Structures like the Taj Mahal and Colosseum are not only artistic marvels but also historical treasures.

1. The Great Wall of China

A series of fortifications built across the historical northern borders of ancient Chinese states and Imperial China to protect against various nomadic groups from the Eurasian Steppe. It is the longest man-made structure in the world, stretching over 21,000 km. It’s visible from low Earth orbit, although that fact is often debated.

Learn more about the Great Wall on Wikipedia.

2. Petra, Jordan

An ancient city in Jordan, famous for its rock-cut architecture and advanced water conduit system. Built possibly as early as 312 BC, Petra showcases ancient engineering brilliance in the heart of the desert.

Learn more about Petra on Wikipedia.

3. The Colosseum, Italy

An oval amphitheatre in the centre of Rome, built of limestone, tuff, and concrete. It held up to 80,000 spectators who gathered to watch gladiator battles and dramas. Although partially ruined, it's a powerful symbol of ancient Rome and architectural mastery.

Learn more about the Colosseum on Wikipedia.

4. Chichén Itzá, Mexico

A pre-Columbian city built by the Maya people. The Temple of Kukulcán casts a serpent shadow during equinoxes — a testament to astronomical precision. Structures like El Castillo show the integration of mathematics, culture, and astronomy in its design.

Learn more about Chichén Itzá on Wikipedia.

5. Machu Picchu, Peru

A 15th century Inca citadel located high in the Andes Mountains. Machu Picchu demonstrates incredible construction using dry-stone walls without mortar. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a marvel of ancient civil engineering.

Learn more about Machu Picchu on Wikipedia.

6. Taj Mahal, India

An ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It features symmetrical gardens, domes, and calligraphy-inlaid marble walls. The monument stands as a symbol of eternal love.

Formula used in construction mix: H2O + CaCO3 (limestone).

Learn more about the Taj Mahal on Wikipedia.

7. Christ the Redeemer, Brazil

A massive Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, standing at 30 meters tall. It’s made of reinforced concrete and soapstone, weighing over 635 tonnes. The view from the top captures the vibrant city below and the lush landscape.

Learn more about Christ the Redeemer on Wikipedia.

These wonders stand as testaments to human creativity and perseverance across the ages.
